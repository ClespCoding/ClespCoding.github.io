A Forgotten Maritime Empire

The North Sea Empire, also known as the Anglo-Scandinavian Empire, was the territory ruled by Cnut the Great as King of England, Denmark, and Norway in the 11th century. This historical period marks a fascinating intersection of cultures that would shape European history for centuries to come.

Our project aims to explore this rich history and its connections to later developments like the Hanseatic League, the Protestant Reformation, and the emergence of modern Northern European states.
