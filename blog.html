Origins of the North Sea Empire

The North Sea Empire, also known as the Anglo-Scandinavian Empire, was the thalassocratic domain ruled by Cnut the Great as King of England, Denmark, and Norway in the early 11th century.

The Hanseatic League's Influence on Northern Europe

The Hanseatic League was a commercial and defensive confederation of merchant guilds and market towns that dominated trade along the coast of Northern Europe from the 13th to the 17th centuries.
